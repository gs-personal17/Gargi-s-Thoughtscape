Watching Life from a Cab Window

I’m a corporate slave now, working from 7:45 to 4:30. I wake up at 6:15 and rush to catch my cab around 7.Every day, on my way to the office, I notice people. It’s kind of my hobby (yeah, I people watch like it’s a sport). When I was a kid, I used to wake up at 5:45. My school bus would arrive around 6:35 to 6:40 AM. I always loved going to school, no matter how early I had to wake up. And now? I struggle to even get up at 6:15. What a plot twist. That contrast sparked a thought in my head, the duality of life. While sitting in the cab, I see kids heading to school, parents dropping them off at the bus stop. At the same time, I see adults heading to their offices, either in cabs like me or on their own vehicles. And I can’t help but think, we’re kind of doing the same thing we did as kids, just with fancier clothes and worse sleep. As kids, we thought being an adult would magically change everything. But it doesn’t. Sometimes, I see a little boy crying because he doesn’t want to go to school. And sometimes, I see another kid who’s excited, smiling, maybe for a field trip or just to meet his friends. At that age, we don’t really understand the importance of these everyday routines. Now, I feel like almost every employee drags themselves to work without much enthusiasm. It’s just a bigger version of school, with more coffee and less recess. Another thing I love seeing in the morning? Old people out for a walk or chilling with a newspaper outside their homes. Sometimes, they sit together, sip hot tea, and chat with their friends. That’s the third stage of life, isn’t it? I wonder if they look at us, rushing to work, and at the kids waiting for school buses, and think,“Yep… those were the days I used to hustle." Now, they seem at peace. Maybe because they’ve already fought their battles. And I hope, someday, when I’m 70, I’ll be able to see this same picture and say,“Yeah, I imagined this back when I was 25.”
